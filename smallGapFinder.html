Minecraft 1.8.9 Small Gap Finder

Intro

In Minecraft 1.8.9, the player's position on the following tick is calculated in the sequence Y-X-Z as follows, stopping when it detects a block collision for each respective dimension/direction. After that, all coordinates are updated simultaneously. A player will land inside a gap only if their y coordinate on the tick before landing (before detecting a vertical block collision) is such that the player model is able to translate into the gap without intersecting any block. This is because the player's y position is calculated first, and then the x and z positions are next. If the player will collide with a block in the x/z direction as simulated from the positional calculations (because of the y coordinate being too low/high for the player model to translate into without intersection), then the player's x/z coordinates will only be updated such that they will be touching the wall. Sadly, this is not enough for the player to actually land inside the gap, and they will simply fall past. The positional coordinates on the next tick are recalculated based on the velocities of the player in each dimension, and the same process of Y-X-Z repeats. Unless a player's y coordinate is within a very specific range on the tick(s) before landing, they will not be able to land inside the gap.

This tool allows you to find small gaps that the player can land in after jumping, blipping, walking off, and etc.

*"Small" gaps are those that are less than 2b (two blocks).*


How-To Tutorial:

  1. Record yourself jumping, walking off, or blipping from your desired starting point.
  2. Take note of the y coordinate at peak height. For jumping, this is after 5 ticks. For walking off, this the y coordinate of the top of the block.
  3. Type that y coordinate into the input box below and click the button or press Enter.
  4. Check the fourth and fifth collumn. Build the setup according to the rounded y coordinate, which is the top of the bottom block.
